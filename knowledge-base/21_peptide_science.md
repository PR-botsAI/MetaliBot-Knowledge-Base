# 🧬 Peptide Science — Complete Guide

> Peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as biological signals — instructing the body to repair, regenerate, burn fat, build muscle, regulate hormones, and heal tissues. This guide covers the most clinically relevant peptides for metabolic health, body composition, recovery, and longevity.

---

## What Are Peptides?

Peptides are molecules made of 2–50 amino acids linked in a chain. They differ from proteins (which are longer chains) in that they are small enough to be recognized as specific biological signals.

**How they work:**
- They bind to specific receptors on cell surfaces
- They trigger precise cellular responses (growth, repair, fat burning, inflammation reduction)
- They mimic or amplify naturally occurring signals in the body
- They are generally well-tolerated because the body already produces similar molecules

**Why peptides matter for metabolic health:**
Many metabolic dysfunctions involve signaling failures — the body isn't producing enough of a repair or regulatory signal. Peptides can restore or amplify these signals with high specificity and minimal side effects compared to pharmaceutical alternatives.

## Category 1: Growth Hormone Secretagogues (GHS)

These peptides stimulate the pituitary gland to release more growth hormone (GH). GH declines dramatically with age — this is a primary driver of aging, fat accumulation, and muscle loss.

### BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)
**Source:** Derived from a protein found in gastric juice
**Primary function:** Systemic healing and tissue repair

**What it does:**
- Accelerates healing of tendons, ligaments, muscles, and bones
- Repairs gut lining (leaky gut / intestinal permeability)
- Reduces inflammation systemically
- Promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation in damaged tissue)
- Protects and heals the stomach and intestines
- Has neuroprotective effects (brain and nerve repair)
- Shown to restore dopamine and serotonin function

**Best for:**
- Joint injuries (tendon/ligament tears)
- Gut issues (Crohn's, IBS, leaky gut)
- Post-surgical healing
- Chronic pain and inflammation
- Brain fog / neurological recovery
- Alcohol-induced organ damage (gastric protection)

**Protocols:**
- Standard dose: 200–500 mcg/day
- Injection: subcutaneous (under skin) OR intramuscular near injury site
- Oral: 500 mcg capsule (less bioavailable but works for gut issues)
- Duration: 4–12 weeks
- Can be used continuously — no receptor downregulation observed

**Stack with:** TB-500 for enhanced tissue repair. Magnesium for anti-inflammation synergy.

**Primary function:** Systemic tissue repair and mobility restoration

**What it does:**

- Reduces inflammation in injured areas
- Improves flexibility and range of motion

**Best for:**

**Protocol:**

### Ipamorelin + CJC-1295
**Type:** Growth hormone releasing peptide (GHRP) + Growth hormone releasing hormone (GHRH)
**Used together** as the most popular GH optimization stack

**Ipamorelin:**
- Selectively stimulates GH release without raising cortisol or prolactin (unlike older GHRPs)
- Most "clean" GHRP available
- Mimics natural GH pulsatility

**CJC-1295:**
- Extends the half-life of natural GHRH signaling
- When combined with Ipamorelin: creates a larger, more sustained GH pulse

**What the combination does:**
- Increases GH and IGF-1 levels
- Promotes fat loss (especially visceral/abdominal fat)
- Builds lean muscle mass
- Improves sleep quality (GH is primarily released during deep sleep)
- Accelerates recovery from exercise
- Anti-aging effects (skin quality, collagen, energy)
- Improves insulin sensitivity

**Protocols:**
- Standard: 100–300 mcg each, combined in one injection
- Timing: Before sleep (maximizes natural GH pulse during sleep)
- Or: Before workout + before sleep
- Cycle: 3 months on, 1 month off
- Subcutaneous injection

**Metabolic synergy:** This stack directly complements the metabolic health approach — GH promotes fat burning and muscle building, exactly what insulin resistance treatment aims to achieve.

**Type:** Synthetic GHRH analog
**Primary function:** More gradual, sustained GH optimization (preferred for older adults)

**What it does:**

## Category 2: Fat Loss Peptides

### AOD-9604
**Type:** Modified fragment of human growth hormone
**Primary function:** Fat burning WITHOUT the anabolic (muscle-building) effects of full GH

**What it does:**
- Directly stimulates fat breakdown (lipolysis) — especially in abdominal fat
- Does NOT raise blood glucose (unlike full HGH)
- Does NOT cause insulin resistance
- Safe for diabetics and metabolically compromised individuals
- Has cartilage-protective properties

**Best for:**
- Stubborn abdominal fat that won't respond to diet
- Metabolically compromised individuals who cannot use full GH protocols
- Diabetics wanting fat loss support

**Protocol:**
- 300 mcg/day, subcutaneous
- Fasted state (morning) for maximum fat mobilization
- 12-week cycles

### MOTS-c
**Type:** Mitochondrial-derived peptide
**Primary function:** Metabolic regulation at the cellular level

**What it does:**
- Activates AMPK (the body's master metabolic switch)
- Improves insulin sensitivity directly
- Promotes fat burning
- Increases exercise capacity and endurance
- Anti-aging effects at the mitochondrial level
- Reduces obesity-associated inflammation

**Why it matters for metabolic health:**
MOTS-c directly addresses insulin resistance at the mitochondrial level — the root of Type 2 diabetes. It's one of the most promising peptides for metabolic disease.

**Protocol:**
- 5–10 mg, 2–3 times per week
- Subcutaneous or IV
- Can be used continuously

### Epithalon (Epitalon)
**Type:** Synthetic tetrapeptide
**Primary function:** Telomere elongation and longevity

**What it does:**
- Activates telomerase (enzyme that lengthens telomeres)
- Telomeres shorten with aging — longer telomeres = slower biological aging
- Regulates melatonin production (improves sleep)
- Antioxidant and anti-tumor properties
- Improves age-related hormonal decline

**Protocol:**
- 5–10 mg/day for 10–20 days
- 1–2 cycles per year
- Subcutaneous or IV

## Important Notes on Peptide Use

**Storage:**
- Most peptides require refrigeration (2–8°C) once reconstituted
- Dry powder is stable at room temperature for months
- Reconstitute with bacteriostatic water
- Use within 30 days once reconstituted

**Administration:**
- Subcutaneous: pinch skin (usually abdomen), inject at 45° angle
- Rotate injection sites to prevent tissue buildup
- Use insulin syringes (29–31 gauge)

**Quality:**
- Source from reputable peptide companies that provide third-party purity testing
- Look for ≥99% purity certificates
- Avoid unverified sources

**Safety:**
- Most peptides have excellent safety profiles
- Not FDA approved for human use in most countries (sold as research chemicals)
- Consult with a physician, especially if diabetic, have cancer history, or are pregnant
- GH-stimulating peptides are not for children or adolescents still growing

**Peptides + Metabolic Health Protocol:**
Peptides work BEST when the foundational metabolic work is done first:
- 3×1 diet reduces the glucose/insulin load that blocks GH and fat burning
- Magnesium + potassium optimize the cellular environment for peptide action
- Candida cleanse removes the toxin load that blunts receptor sensitivity
- Intermittent fasting amplifies GH release and fat burning that peptides support
