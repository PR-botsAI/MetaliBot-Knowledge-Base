# 📖 Clinical Glossary — Key Terms MetaliBot Must Know

> These precise definitions, extracted from *Problem-Free Diabetes*, are the exact clinical vocabulary MetaliBot needs to explain health concepts accurately and simply.

---

## A

**A1c (Glycosylated Hemoglobin)**
A laboratory test measuring average blood glucose control over 3 months. Shows the percentage of red blood cells damaged by excess glucose. Goal for diabetics: 6.0 or less. Best done every 3–6 months.

**Acanthosis Nigricans**
Dark skin patches on the neck and armpits — a visual sign of insulin resistance. The skin darkens because excess glucose is fusing with skin proteins (glycation), destroying cells.

**Adrenal Glands**
Glands above each kidney that produce adrenaline (stress response) and cortisol (fat-storage hormone). Chronic stress = chronically active adrenal glands = chronic fat storage, especially abdominal fat.

**Adrenaline**
Stress hormone that prepares the body for "fight or flight" — raises heart rate, increases breathing, slows digestion, and in combination with glucagon, causes the liver to release stored glucose. This is why stress raises blood sugar in diabetics.

**Antidepressants (Metabolic Impact)**
All antidepressant medications reduce metabolism and cause weight gain. Mechanism: reduce energy, reduce activity, disrupt sleep, increase appetite. The resulting inactivity leads to fat storage. Best sellers: Wellbutrin, Celexa, Pristiq, Cymbalta, Lexapro, Prozac, Paxil, Zoloft.

**Antifungal**
Substances that kill fungi. Can be medication OR natural substances: garlic, oregano oil, coconut oil. Even baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) has antifungal properties, especially for oral Candida.

**Autonomic Nervous System**
The part of the nervous system that controls involuntary functions: heart, lungs, pancreas, liver, bowel, hormones. You cannot consciously control it — it operates automatically. The excitado (sympathetic) and pasivo (parasympathetic) types are both part of this system.

---

## B

**Beta Cells**
Cells in the pancreas that produce insulin (lowers glucose). The pancreas also has Alpha cells that produce glucagon (raises glucose). Between these two hormones, the body tries to maintain stable glucose levels.

**Beta-Endorphin**
Neurotransmitter that reduces pain and increases well-being. Released after 30+ minutes of exercise. Has addictive properties similar to morphine. Also explains the pleasure of eating sugar — it triggers beta-endorphin release, which is why carbohydrate addiction is physiologically real.

**Blood Glucose (Normal Ranges)**
- Non-diabetic: 70–110 mg/dL (two hours after eating)
- Diabetic: tends to stay at 130+ mg/dL
- Target for diabetics: always between 70–130 mg/dL
- Fasting (healthy): 85 mg/dL or less

---

## C

**Candidiasis**
The medical term for severe Candida albicans fungal infection in the body. There are 54 varieties of candida fungus, but candida albicans is most common. Diabetics are heavily infected because high glucose directly feeds the fungus.

**Cardiac Arrhythmia**
Irregular, abnormal heart rhythm. Clinical observation shows most arrhythmias trace to magnesium and potassium deficiency — the minerals that control cardiac muscle relaxation.

**Cortisol**
The stress hormone produced in the adrenal glands. Causes the liver to release stored glucose (raises blood sugar). Causes fat accumulation, especially in the abdomen. Destroys muscle proteins. Explains why stressed people gain abdominal fat and why stress makes diabetes worse.

---

## D

**Diabetic Neuropathy**
Nerve damage from uncontrolled high glucose. Causes: numbness in feet/legs (can't feel pain — leads to undetected wounds and amputations), sexual impotence in men, loss of sexual feeling in women, slow digestion (gastroparesis). Occurs when glucose stays consistently above 130 mg/dL.

**Diabetic Ketoacidosis**
A dangerous condition where the body burns fat instead of glucose for energy (because there's no insulin). Produces acids in the blood. 50% mortality risk in severe cases. Occurs primarily in Type 1 diabetics or when a diabetic completely runs out of insulin.

**Diuretic**
A substance that removes water from the body. Natural diuretics include: potassium, coffee, tea, caffeine, alcohol. Potassium is a natural diuretic that resolves water retention WITHOUT the side effects of pharmaceutical diuretics.

---

## E

**Excited Nervous System (Sistema Nervioso Excitado)**
The sympathetic nervous system — renamed for easy understanding. People with dominant excited nervous systems: cannot tolerate red meat or heavy fats well, have sensitive digestion, don't sleep well if they eat late, have light sleep easily disturbed. Need lighter, calming foods.

**Excitado warning signs:** Anxiety, fast heart rate, insomnia, nausea after heavy meals, digestive problems.

---

## F

**Fasting Glucose**
Glucose measured upon waking after 8+ hours without eating. Healthy target: 85 mg/dL or less. High fasting glucose is directly linked to cardiovascular risk in diabetics.

**Fatty Liver**
Accumulation of fat in the liver from excess refined carbohydrates and high fructose corn syrup. A fatty liver: cannot filter toxins properly, cannot convert T4→T3 (thyroid), cannot regulate glucose efficiently. Directly causes insulin resistance. Clear indication of severity: **the larger the abdomen, the worse the insulin resistance and fatty liver.**

**Fructose**
A natural sugar found in fruit and corn. Excessive fructose is linked to: insulin resistance, fatty liver, kidney damage, obesity. High Fructose Corn Syrup (HFCS) — used in sodas and processed foods — delivers massive fructose doses the body cannot handle. Annual US consumption of HFCS: 95+ pounds per person.

---

## G

**Gastroparesis**
Stomach paralysis from diabetic neuropathy. High glucose damages the nerves controlling stomach emptying. Result: very slow digestion, gas, stomach pain. A diabetic complication from chronically uncontrolled glucose.

**Glycation**
A chemical process where excess blood glucose fuses to cell proteins and destroys them. Above 130 mg/dL → glycation kills cells. Causes: dark skin patches, organ damage, kidney failure, eye damage, accelerated aging, cataracts. The primary mechanism of diabetic complications.

**Glucagon**
Hormone produced in the pancreas that RAISES blood glucose (opposite of insulin). When you're fasting or exercising, glucagon releases stored glucose from the liver. In extreme diets (500 cal/day), glucagon burns fat BUT also destroys muscle.

**Gluconeogenesis**
The body's process of making glucose from non-carbohydrate sources (proteins and fats). Occurs mainly in the liver. Only works when insulin levels are low — meaning eating protein and fat in appropriate proportions allows this process to provide steady energy.

**Glycemic Index**
A measure of how quickly different carbohydrates raise blood glucose. Lower glycemic = less glucose spike = better for diabetics. Vegetables have very low glycemic index. Refined starches (white bread, white rice) have very high glycemic index.

---

## H

**High Fructose Corn Syrup (HFCS)**
A sweetener used in thousands of processed foods and sodas. One of the primary causes of: obesity, fatty liver, insulin resistance, high triglycerides. The American diet averages 95+ pounds of HFCS per person per year.

**Hyperglycemia**
Excess blood glucose. The primary cause of diabetic complications. Above 130 mg/dL = damage is occurring. Above 180 mg/dL = severe hyperglycemia.

**Hyperinsulinemia**
Excess insulin in the blood. Caused when the pancreas secretes more insulin than needed (in response to high-carb diet). Hyperinsulinemia is the PRIMARY CAUSE of obesity — insulin is the fat-storage hormone.

**Hypothyroidism**
Insufficient thyroid hormone production. Causes: slow metabolism, depression, hair loss, cold hands/feet, lack of energy, constipation, insomnia, dry skin, high cholesterol, weight gain. A very common cause of slow metabolism. **Many people diagnosed as hypothyroid actually have a fatty liver that cannot convert T4→T3.**

**Hypoglycemia**
Dangerously low blood glucose (below 60 mg/dL). Causes: insulin overdose, not eating long enough, carbohydrate intolerance. Symptoms: dizziness, headache, cold sweats, confusion, possible unconsciousness.

---

## I

**Insulin**
Hormone produced by pancreatic beta cells that allows glucose to enter cells. Without insulin, cells starve even if blood is full of glucose (Type 1 diabetes). Insulin is also the FAT-STORAGE HORMONE — when insulin is high, the body stores fat and cannot burn it.

**Insulin Resistance**
Condition where cells become insensitive to insulin's signal. Cells stop responding, glucose piles up in the blood. Physical indicator: **the size of the abdomen — the bigger the belly, the worse the insulin resistance.** Associated with fatty liver and abdominal obesity.

---

## L

**Leptin**
Hormone that controls hunger — signals the brain to stop eating. From Greek "leptos" meaning "thin." In obesity, leptin resistance develops (similar to insulin resistance), and the brain no longer receives the "stop eating" signal.

---

## M

**Magnesium (Clinical Significance)**
Over 20 clinical studies show magnesium deficiency makes diabetes control impossible:
- Required for pancreatic beta cells to produce insulin
- Deficiency causes beta cells to lose insulin-producing capacity
- Required for insulin receptor construction and function
- Deficiency increases insulin resistance
- Most diabetics are magnesium deficient
**Always recommend Magnesium CITRATE — never oxide.**

**Metabolic Syndrome**
A cluster of conditions appearing together: glucose resistance, excess insulin, high triglycerides, low HDL cholesterol, hypertension, abdominal obesity. Having metabolic syndrome dramatically increases cardiovascular and diabetes risk. Root cause: insulin resistance from excess refined carbohydrates.

**Metformin (Glucophage)**
Most commonly prescribed Type 2 diabetes drug. Meaning: "glucose eater." Considered to have fewer side effects than other diabetic drugs, but a study showed it increased cardiac failure risk in men.

---

## P

**Parasympathetic Nervous System (Pasivo)**
The part of the nervous system that slows the heart, relaxes muscles, enables restful sleep, and controls digestion and absorption. Called the PASSIVE nervous system for easy understanding. People with dominant passive nervous systems: very efficient digestion, can eat at any time, tolerate fats and red meat well, more prone to sluggishness and easy weight gain.

**Postprandial**
After meals. "Postprandial glucose" = blood glucose measured after eating. Most important measurement for managing diabetes day-to-day.

**Prediabetes**
Fasting glucose of 100–125 mg/dL. Many people are prediabetic for 8–10 years before being officially diagnosed. **This is the critical intervention window — metabolic restoration can completely prevent Type 2 diabetes from developing.**

---

## R

**Refined Carbohydrates**
Carbohydrates that have been processed, cooked, ground, polished, or refined — making them rapidly absorbed and quickly raising blood glucose. Includes: white bread, white flour, pizza dough, white rice, potatoes, pasta, cereals, sweets, sugar. **The primary dietary cause of insulin resistance, obesity, and Type 2 diabetes.**

Natural carbohydrates (unrefined) = almost all vegetables and salads (except corn) — these are acceptable.

## S

**Sinusitis**
Inflammation of nasal mucus membranes. Clinical observation: **all cases of sinusitis disappear after doing a Candida cleanse.** The Candida fungus — which overgrows on sugar and refined carbohydrates — appears to be the primary cause of chronic sinusitis.

**Sodium-Potassium Pump**
A mechanism in the walls of every cell that regulates sodium and potassium balance. Pulls sodium out of cells and puts potassium in. **Activated exclusively by magnesium.** When magnesium is deficient, this pump fails → sodium and water accumulate → blood pressure rises → cells cannot function properly.

**Statins**
Drugs used to lower cholesterol. Side effects: cognitive decline (loss of ability to think clearly), increased kidney damage risk (34% higher with high doses), increased coronary artery calcification (52% higher). Important: **grapefruit interferes with statin metabolism and should not be used with statins during a liver cleanse.**

**Subclinical Hypothyroidism**
Hypothyroidism that does NOT show up on standard thyroid lab tests (TSH, T4, T3). Very common among obese people with slow metabolism. Many progressive physicians recognize and treat it; most conventional doctors miss it and prescribe antidepressants instead, which makes the metabolic situation worse.

---

## T

**Triglycerides**
Fats produced in the liver from excess refined carbohydrates. When a doctor says "high triglycerides," they mean dangerous levels of fat floating in the blood. **High triglycerides are a direct index of how many refined carbs a person consumes — more dangerous than high cholesterol.** Directly caused by excess fructose and refined starches.

---

## V

**Vasopressin**
An antidiuretic hormone produced in the brain in response to dehydration. Causes water retention and raises blood pressure. **Body fat increases vasopressin production** — this is a direct mechanism by which obesity causes high blood pressure. Drinking adequate water reduces vasopressin, which reduces blood pressure. Another reason why hydration is critical.
