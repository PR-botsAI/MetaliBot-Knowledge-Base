# 🚨 Food Aggressors — The TAM Framework

## What Is a Food Aggressor?

A food aggressor is any food that triggers a **negative biological reaction** in a SPECIFIC individual's body. There is no universal list — every metabolism is unique.

Key insight: A food can be healthy for most people and an aggressor for you.

## The TAM — 3 Most Common Aggressors

Based on clinical observation across tens of thousands of cases:

| Letter | Spanish | English | Common Sources |
|--------|---------|---------|----------------|
| **T** | Trigo | Wheat | Bread, pasta, cookies, flour tortillas, baked goods |
| **A** | Arroz | Rice | White rice, rice products |
| **M** | Maíz | Corn | Corn tortillas, cereals, chips, corn syrup, processed foods |

## Other Frequently Observed Aggressors

- **Pasteurized cow's milk** and dairy products
- **Soy** and soy derivatives (including soy oil)
- **Shellfish** — especially shrimp, for many people
- **Nightshade vegetables** (tomato, eggplant, bell pepper) — in sensitive individuals
- **Gluten-containing grains** (wheat, barley, rye)
- **Eggs** — a smaller percentage of people
- **Excess coffee/caffeine**

## Two Science-Based Identification Methods

### Method 1: The Glucometer Test

1. Measure fasting glucose **before** eating a specific food
2. Eat ONLY that food
3. Measure glucose again **1 hour later**
4. **If glucose rises MORE than 12 points → that food is an aggressor for YOUR body**

This gives you objective, personalized data about YOUR specific metabolism.

### Method 2: The Elimination Protocol

1. **Remove** the suspected food completely for 2–3 weeks
2. Observe if symptoms improve
3. **Reintroduce** the food
4. If symptoms return → aggressor confirmed

## Results When Aggressors Are Removed

Typically observed within **2–4 weeks**:

✅ Rapid abdominal fat loss
✅ Bloating and gas disappear
✅ Sleep quality improves significantly
✅ Energy levels increase
✅ Chronic headaches resolve
✅ Skin clears (acne, eczema, rashes)
✅ Blood pressure drops
✅ Blood sugar improves in diabetics

## Important Notes

- Start with TAM (wheat, rice, corn) — most people find at least one
- Remove one at a time to identify which specific food is the aggressor
- Some aggressors cause inflammation (weight, pain) while others cause digestive issues
- What's an aggressor today may not always be one if the gut heals
