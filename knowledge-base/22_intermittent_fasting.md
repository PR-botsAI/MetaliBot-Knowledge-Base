## What Is Intermittent Fasting?

Intermittent fasting means cycling between periods of eating and periods of not eating. The science: your body has two primary metabolic states:

**FED STATE (0–4 hours after eating):**
- Insulin is high
- Body is storing and processing nutrients
- Fat burning is OFF (high insulin blocks fat release from cells)
- Body uses glucose as primary fuel

**FASTED STATE (12+ hours after last meal):**
- Insulin is low
- Body switches to burning stored fat for fuel
- Growth hormone spikes (GH increases 300–2000% during extended fasting)
- Autophagy activates (cellular cleanup and repair)
- Ketones rise (brain runs on ketones — clarity improves)
- Inflammation decreases

**The problem with the standard 3-meals-a-day eating pattern:**
You eat at 7am, 12pm, 6pm + snacks. Your insulin is elevated most of the day. You spend almost no time in the fasted state. Fat burning barely occurs. The body never has time to clean up damaged cells.

## The Main Intermittent Fasting Protocols

### 16:8 (Most Popular — Best Starting Point)
**Eat:** 8-hour window (e.g., 12pm–8pm)
**Fast:** 16 hours

**Why it works:**
- Easy to implement (just skip breakfast)
- 12+ hours of fasting state achieved daily
- Insulin drops significantly in the fasting window
- Fat burning activates reliably
- Sustainable as a lifestyle

**Best schedule options:**
- 12pm–8pm (skip breakfast, eat lunch and dinner)
- 10am–6pm (late breakfast, early dinner)
- 1pm–9pm (late lunch and dinner)

**Who it's best for:** Beginners, people with busy mornings, metabolic health improvement goals.

---

### 18:6
**Eat:** 6-hour window
**Fast:** 18 hours

**Additional benefits over 16:8:**
- More significant autophagy activation
- Greater GH release
- Deeper ketone production
- Better for stubborn fat loss

**Typical schedule:** 1pm–7pm or 2pm–8pm

---

### 20:4 ("Warrior Diet")
**Eat:** 4-hour window
**Fast:** 20 hours

**Benefits:**
- Strong autophagy and cellular cleanup
- Significant GH spike
- Powerful for fat loss
- Some people feel most energized on this protocol

**Challenge:** Eating sufficient calories and nutrients in a 4-hour window. Not ideal for those with high caloric needs.

---

### OMAD (One Meal a Day)
**Eat:** 1 meal per day (usually 1-hour window)
**Fast:** 23 hours

**Benefits:**
- Maximum autophagy
- Very strong GH response
- Simplicity (one meal to plan)
- Powerful for metabolic reset

**Cautions:**
- Harder to meet nutritional needs
- Can stress adrenal glands long-term
- Not recommended for beginners or people with metabolic disease without supervision
- Timing the meal properly matters significantly

---

### 5:2 Protocol
**Normal eating:** 5 days/week
**Restricted eating:** 2 non-consecutive days (500–600 calories only)

**Benefits:**
- Easier social flexibility
- Similar metabolic benefits to daily IF
- Good for people who struggle with daily eating windows

---

### Extended Fasting (24–72 hours)
**Used for:** Deep autophagy, gut healing, insulin resistance reversal, metabolic reset

**24-hour fast:**
- Complete autophagy activation
- Significant immune system regeneration
- Powerful for breaking a weight loss plateau
- Practical: dinner to dinner

**48–72 hour fast:**
- Maximum autophagy (stem cell regeneration reported)
- Complete gut rest and healing
- Immune system reset
- Requires electrolyte supplementation (magnesium, potassium, sodium)
- Medical supervision recommended for metabolic disease

## What to Consume During the Fasting Window

**Does NOT break the fast (insulin response is negligible):**
- ✅ Water (always — essential during fasting)
- ✅ Black coffee (no milk, no sweetener)
- ✅ Plain green tea
- ✅ Herbal teas (no sweetener)
- ✅ Sparkling water (plain)
- ✅ Salt (electrolytes — especially important for longer fasts)
- ✅ Apple cider vinegar in water (improves insulin sensitivity, does not break fast)

**DOES break the fast (triggers insulin response):**
- ❌ Any calories (even small amounts)
- ❌ Milk or cream in coffee
- ❌ Protein shakes or BCAAs
- ❌ Sweeteners (even stevia can trigger insulin response in some people)
- ❌ Bone broth (has some caloric content)
- ❌ Fruit juices

**Gray area (minimal insulin response, acceptable for most IF goals):**
- ⚡ MCT oil (pure C8) — rapidly converted to ketones, minimal insulin impact, keeps energy up
- ⚡ Black coffee with small amount of coconut oil ("bulletproof light")
- ⚡ Apple cider vinegar shots

## Intermittent Fasting for Diabetics — Special Considerations

**Benefits for Type 2 diabetics:**
- Directly lowers fasting glucose (liver depletes glycogen stores during fast)
- Dramatically improves insulin sensitivity
- Reduces need for diabetes medications over time
- Promotes fat loss from the abdomen (reduces insulin resistance)

**Critical warnings:**
- If taking insulin or sulfonylureas: IF can cause dangerous hypoglycemia
- Never start IF without consulting your doctor if on diabetes medications
- Medications may need to be reduced as IF improves glucose control
- Monitor glucose frequently, especially in the first weeks

**Safe starting approach for diabetics:**
- Begin with 12:12 (12 hours fasting, 12 hours eating)
- Gradually extend to 14:10, then 16:8 over several weeks
- Track glucose carefully throughout
- Work with physician to adjust medications as needed

## Autophagy — The Cellular Cleanup System

Autophagy means "self-eating" — the body's mechanism for cleaning up damaged cell components.

**What autophagy does:**
- Recycles damaged proteins and organelles
- Removes dysfunctional mitochondria
- Clears out misfolded proteins (linked to Alzheimer's and Parkinson's)
- Eliminates intracellular pathogens (including Candida)
- Reduces cancer cell survival
- Literally slows biological aging

**When autophagy activates:**
- Begins around 14–16 hours of fasting
- Peaks at 24–48 hours
- Enhanced by exercise during the fasting state
- Enhanced by coffee (polyphenols stimulate autophagy)
- Suppressed by any caloric intake, protein especially

## Practical Implementation Guide

### Starting IF (Week 1–2)
1. Choose a 16:8 window that fits your life (most people: 12pm–8pm)
2. Drink plenty of water during the fast
3. Black coffee or green tea are your allies in the morning
4. Expect some hunger for the first 3–7 days (it passes)
5. Do NOT restrict calories during the eating window — eat normally

### Managing Hunger
- Most hunger is habitual, not physiological — it passes in 15–20 minutes
- Drink water or coffee when hunger hits during the fast
- ACV shot (see Morning Protocols) dramatically reduces hunger
- After 2 weeks, most people report hunger disappearing during the fast

### Exercise Timing
- **Fasted cardio:** Maximum fat burning — walk or light cardio before breaking the fast
- **Strength training:** Best 1–2 hours before or at the start of eating window (maximize protein use)
- **Peptide-assisted recovery:** Take BPC-157/TB-500 post-workout even in fasted state

### Breaking the Fast — Best Foods
1. Something easy to digest first (not a massive meal)
2. Protein + healthy fat (eggs, avocado) — doesn't spike insulin aggressively
3. Then add vegetables (Type A foods from 3×1 system)
4. Keep the first meal relatively light — your digestive system is waking up
5. Reserve the larger meal for 2–3 hours into the eating window

### Electrolytes During Extended Fasting
For fasts over 16–18 hours, electrolytes become critical:
- Magnesium citrate (MagicMag®): prevents cramps, supports energy
- Potassium: prevents dizziness and muscle weakness
- Sodium: small amount of salt in water maintains blood pressure
- These do NOT break the fast
