# 🌡️ Hypothyroidism — The Underdiagnosed Metabolic Enemy

> One of the most frequently missed causes of slow metabolism. Metabolic science addresses thyroid health differently from conventional medicine.

---

## The Thyroid's Role in Metabolism

The thyroid gland is the body's **metabolic thermostat** — it controls the speed at which all metabolic processes run.

Thyroid hormones:
- **T4 (thyroxine)** — Produced by the thyroid gland. The storage form.
- **T3 (triiodothyronine)** — The ACTIVE form. Drives energy production and fat burning.
- **Conversion:** T4 must be converted to T3 in the liver. **A fatty liver cannot do this conversion.** This is the critical link most doctors miss.

---

## Standard Lab Tests Miss Most Hypothyroidism

Standard thyroid panel (TSH, T4, T3) often comes back "normal" in people who have clear hypothyroid symptoms:

**Subclinical hypothyroidism** = hypothyroid symptoms WITHOUT abnormal lab results.

Common scenario:
1. Patient has: fatigue, weight gain, cold extremities, hair loss, depression, constipation
2. Doctor tests TSH — comes back "normal"
3. Doctor prescribes antidepressants
4. Antidepressants slow metabolism further
5. Patient gets worse

**The metabolic approach:** These symptoms indicate a slow metabolism regardless of what lab tests show. Address the underlying causes (fatty liver, nutrient deficiencies) rather than chasing lab numbers.

## The Temperature Test — A Simple Home Test

Body temperature is a direct indicator of thyroid and metabolic function:

**How to do it:**
1. Place a thermometer in your armpit before getting out of bed in the morning (do not get up first — movement raises temperature)
2. Keep it there for 10 minutes
3. Record the temperature for 3 consecutive days

**Interpretation:**
| Temperature | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| 97.8°F–98.2°F (36.6–36.8°C) | Normal thyroid function |
| Below 97.8°F (36.6°C) | Possible hypothyroidism |
| Consistently below 97.0°F (36.1°C) | Significant hypothyroid indication |

**Women:** Take measurements on days 2, 3, and 4 of the menstrual cycle when temperature is most stable.

## Hypothyroid Symptoms to Watch For

**Physical:**
- Cold hands and feet (even in warm weather)
- Hair loss (especially when combing — more than normal shedding)
- Dry skin
- Constipation
- Weight gain despite normal eating
- Slow pulse
- Puffiness (especially face and ankles)

**Energy/Mental:**
- Chronic fatigue that doesn't improve with rest
- Depression
- Brain fog
- Lack of motivation
- Poor concentration

**Metabolic:**
- High cholesterol (thyroid regulates cholesterol metabolism)
- Slow digestion
- Low body temperature
- Difficulty losing weight despite dietary changes

## Goitrogens — Foods That Block Thyroid Function

Certain foods contain **goitrogens** — substances that interfere with thyroid hormone production:

**High-goitrogen foods (consume in moderation, especially if hypothyroid):**
- Soy and soy products (among the strongest goitrogens)
- Raw cruciferous vegetables in large amounts (broccoli, cauliflower, kale, cabbage) — cooking reduces goitrogenic effect significantly
- Corn (also contains natural goitrogens)
- Yuca/cassava (also naturally contains cyanide compounds affecting thyroid)

**Important note:** These foods are generally healthy and don't need to be avoided entirely — they're only a concern when consumed in large daily quantities by hypothyroid individuals. Cooking largely neutralizes the goitrogenic effect.

## Medications That Suppress Thyroid Function

- **Fluoride** (in water and toothpaste) — chemically similar to iodine, competes with it
- **Some antidepressants** — can suppress thyroid function
- **Lithium** (used in bipolar disorder) — well-documented thyroid suppressor
- **Amiodarone** (heart medication) — contains large amounts of iodine, disrupts thyroid
