## What Is Metabolism — The Simple Definition

> *"Metabolism: the sum of all the movements, actions and changes that occur in the body to convert food and nutrients into energy for survival."*

All body processes involve movement: digestion, absorption, respiration, immune system, circulation, elimination. Movement requires energy. When the metabolism is **slow**, all these processes run slowly — this manifests as constipation, toxin accumulation, poor circulation, frequent infections, poor digestion, obesity.

**"Slow metabolism"** simply means the body's processes are not running at optimal speed.

## The 12 Enemies of Metabolism

Clinical observation identifies these as the primary factors that slow or damage metabolic function:

1. **Excess sugars and refined carbohydrates** — #1 enemy. Creates glucose spikes, insulin floods, fat storage, Candida overgrowth.

2. **Dehydration** — Most people are chronically dehydrated. Formula: weight in lbs ÷ 2 = oz of water needed daily.

3. **Thyroid gland dysfunction** — Hypothyroidism (often subclinical, missed by standard tests). Body temperature test: measure armpit temperature before getting out of bed. Normal: 97.8°F–98.2°F. Below 97.8°F = possible hypothyroidism.

4. **Stress and cortisol** — Cortisol is a fat-storage hormone. Chronic stress = chronic fat accumulation, especially abdominal. Physiologically documented to cause weight gain.

5. **Poor digestion** — Undigested food cannot nourish cells. Creates toxins, inflammation, poor nutrient absorption.

6. **Enemy substances** — Certain artificial sweeteners, preservatives, food dyes, industrial seed oils.

7. **Food intolerances** — Individual reactions to specific foods that create inflammation and slow metabolism. TAM (Wheat, Rice, Corn) are most common.

8. **Poor breakfast** — Skipping breakfast or eating a sugary breakfast starts the day with an insulin spike that sets the metabolic tone for the entire day.

9. **Candida Albicans — "The Silent Epidemic"** — Affects the vast majority of overweight people and almost no one knows it. Candida toxins create systemic inflammation that blocks fat burning, worsens insulin resistance, and drives carbohydrate cravings.

10. **Hormonal imbalances** — Female hormones significantly affect metabolism. Estrogen dominance, PCOS, and progesterone deficiency all slow metabolic function.

11. **Antidepressants and metabolism-slowing medications** — Documented metabolic impact through reduced energy, increased appetite, disrupted sleep, and lowered activity.

12. **Sedentary lifestyle** — Lack of movement directly slows all metabolic processes. Muscles are the primary fat-burning tissue — they must be used to function.

## Losing Weight vs. Getting Slim — A Critical Distinction

This is one of the most important concepts in the metabolic approach:

**"Bajar de peso" (Losing weight)** = Losing any mass — muscle, water, bone, or fat. This is what crash diets achieve. The scale goes down but body composition worsens because the tissue that burns fat has been destroyed.

**"Adelgazar" (Getting slim / burning fat)** = Losing specifically FAT while maintaining or building muscle. This requires:
- Adequate protein intake (preserves muscle)
- Moderate carbohydrate reduction (reduces insulin, enables fat burning)
- Proper hydration (enables fat mobilization)
- Adequate minerals (magnesium + potassium)

**The goal is ALWAYS to adelgazar — not just bajar de peso.** This distinction separates the metabolic approach from conventional dieting.

## Carbohydrate Addiction — It's Physiologically Real

Refined carbohydrates are physiologically addictive. The mechanism:

1. Sugar and refined carbohydrates trigger beta-endorphin release in the brain
2. Beta-endorphins are natural morphine-like chemicals that produce pleasure and reduce pain
3. The body becomes dependent on this release
4. Cravings for bread, sweets, sodas = a physiological withdrawal state, not a lack of willpower

**This is why "just eat less" doesn't work.** Asking someone to overcome a physiological addiction with willpower alone fails at the root cause level. The solution is changing diet composition, addressing Candida (which also drives carb cravings), and ensuring adequate minerals.

## The Snacks Strategy (Meriendas)

Proper snacking between meals is important for:
- Preventing blood glucose drops (hypoglycemia between meals)
- Preventing the body from entering starvation mode
- Maintaining consistent energy levels throughout the day

**Good snack choices (all Type A):**
- Nuts (almonds, walnuts, pecans)
- Cheese (any natural cheese)
- Eggs (boiled, deviled)
- Avocado
- Raw vegetables with cheese or protein

**Avoid:** Crackers, chips, fruit juice, granola bars, commercial "healthy" snack bars (usually loaded with sugar or refined grains).

6. **Magnesium/potassium deficiency** — Cells cannot use glucose without these minerals.
7. **Stress/high cortisol** — Cortisol actively prevents fat release from cells.
8. **Poor sleep** — Raises cortisol and disrupts metabolic hormones.
