## AOD-9604
**Full name:** Anti-Obesity Drug 9604  
**Class:** Modified GH fragment (hGH 176-191)

**Mechanism:**
AOD-9604 is a synthetic fragment of human growth hormone specifically engineered to retain GH's fat-burning (lipolytic) properties while eliminating its anabolic (tissue-building) and glucose-raising effects. It stimulates lipolysis (fat breakdown) and inhibits lipogenesis (new fat formation) via beta-3 adrenergic receptor activation — without affecting IGF-1 levels or blood glucose.

**Primary uses:**
- Targeted fat loss, especially visceral and abdominal fat
- Metabolically compromised individuals who cannot safely use full GH protocols
- Type 2 diabetics wanting fat loss support
- Plateau-breaking when diet and exercise stall

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 200–300 mcg/day
Timing: Morning, fasted state (30–60 min before eating or exercise)
Route: Subcutaneous injection (preferred) or sublingual
Cycle: 12 weeks on, 4 weeks off
Storage: Refrigerate once reconstituted; stable 30 days at 4°C
```

**Synergies:**
- + Intermittent fasting: Both activate fat oxidation via overlapping pathways
- + CJC-1295/Ipamorelin before sleep: AOD handles daytime fat burning; GH peptides handle overnight repair and fat burning
- + 3×1 diet: Low insulin from diet allows AOD's lipolytic signal to work without insulin blockade
- + MOTS-c: AMPK activation from MOTS-c enhances fat oxidation that AOD initiates

**Cautions:**
- Generally very well tolerated — one of the safest peptides
- Not a GH secretagogue — does NOT raise IGF-1
- Safe for diabetics (does not affect glucose)

**Metabolic connection:**
AOD-9604 specifically targets the same visceral fat that insulin resistance and cortisol create. It's the surgical tool for belly fat that doesn't respond to diet alone — ideal AFTER the 3×1 diet has reduced insulin baseline.

## ARA-290 (Cibinetide)
**Class:** Erythropoietin-derived peptide (non-erythropoietic)

**Mechanism:**
ARA-290 binds to the innate repair receptor (IRR) — a protective receptor distinct from the erythropoietin receptor. Activates tissue protection, reduces inflammation, promotes nerve repair without stimulating red blood cell production.

**Primary uses:**
- Diabetic neuropathy (nerve repair)
- Chronic inflammation reduction
- Sarcoidosis small fiber neuropathy
- Post-COVID nerve damage

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 4 mcg/kg body weight, 3x/week
Route: Subcutaneous injection
Duration: 12+ weeks for neuropathy applications
```

**Clinical evidence:**
Clinical trials in diabetic peripheral neuropathy show significant improvement in nerve fiber density and pain scores. One of the few peptides with Phase II human trial data specifically for neuropathy.

## BPC-157
**Full name:** Body Protection Compound 157  
**Class:** Gastric pentadecapeptide (15 amino acids)

**Mechanism:**
BPC-157 is derived from a protein found naturally in human gastric juice. It upregulates growth hormone receptors in tendon fibroblasts, promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation), activates nitric oxide synthesis, modulates dopamine and serotonin systems, and protects the gut lining via multiple parallel pathways including FAK-paxillin signaling.

Key molecular targets:
- VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor) upregulation → new blood vessel growth in injured tissue
- NO synthesis → vasodilation and tissue oxygenation
- Dopamine system normalization → neuroprotective effects
- Gut mucosa regeneration via EGF receptor activation

**Primary uses:**
- Tendon and ligament injury healing
- Gut healing (leaky gut, IBD, IBS, gastric ulcers)
- Neurological recovery and neuroprotection
- Systemic inflammation reduction
- Post-surgical recovery
- Muscle tear repair
- Alcohol/NSAID-induced gut damage
- Depression and brain fog (via dopamine system restoration)

**Protocol:**
```
For systemic use (injuries, gut):
Dose: 200–500 mcg/day
Route: Subcutaneous injection (near injury site for local effect)
Timing: Any time (not dependent on fasting state)
Cycle: 4–12 weeks; can be used continuously — no receptor downregulation

For gut-specific use:
Dose: 250–500 mcg oral (capsule or dissolved in water)
Timing: 30 min before eating
Oral bioavailability: Lower than injection but sufficient for GI tract

For injury healing:
Dose: 250 mcg subcutaneous near the injury site
Frequency: Daily or twice daily during acute phase
```

**Synergies:**
- + TB-500: The most powerful healing combination — BPC repairs locally and systemically; TB-500 promotes systemic tissue repair. Together: dramatically faster healing than either alone.
- + KPV: Synergistic gut healing — BPC heals structure; KPV reduces inflammation
- + Collagen + Vitamin C: Provides building materials for the tissue BPC stimulates to grow
- + Magnesium: Anti-inflammatory support enhances BPC healing environment
- + L-Glutamine: Gut cell fuel complements BPC's gut lining repair

**Cautions:**
- One of the most extensively studied and safest peptides available
- No human clinical trials yet (all research in animals) — but mechanism is well understood
- Avoid in active cancer — angiogenesis (blood vessel growth) could potentially support tumor growth
- Extremely well tolerated — minor injection site irritation most common side effect

**Metabolic connection:**
Candida overgrowth causes intestinal permeability (leaky gut). BPC-157 directly repairs the gut lining that Candida damages. It also restores the dopamine system that Candida toxins (acetaldehyde) disrupt — addressing brain fog at a peptide level.

## CJC-1295 (with DAC)
**Full name:** CJC-1295 Drug Affinity Complex  
**Class:** GHRH analog (Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone analog)

**Mechanism:**
CJC-1295 with DAC is a modified GHRH that binds to albumin in the blood, extending its half-life from minutes (natural GHRH) to approximately 6–8 days. It stimulates the pituitary gland to release growth hormone in pulses that more closely mimic natural pulsatile GH release over a sustained period.

**CJC-1295 WITHOUT DAC (also called Mod GRF 1-29):**
- Half-life: ~30 minutes
- Requires more frequent dosing
- Produces a more natural GH pulse when combined with GHRP
- Usually preferred in combination protocols

**Primary uses:**
- Growth hormone optimization without exogenous GH
- Fat loss (especially with Ipamorelin)
- Muscle building and recovery
- Improved sleep quality (GH released during deep sleep)
- Anti-aging
- Injury recovery

**Protocol:**
```
With DAC:
Dose: 1,000–2,000 mcg (1–2 mg) once or twice weekly
Route: Subcutaneous injection
Note: Once-weekly dosing is convenient; less natural pulsatility

Without DAC (Mod GRF 1-29) — PREFERRED in combination:
Dose: 100–300 mcg
Frequency: 2–3x daily (before sleep mandatory; before workout optional)
Combine with: Ipamorelin or GHRP-2/GHRP-6 at same time for synergy
Cycle: 3 months on, 1 month off
```

**Synergies:**
- + Ipamorelin: THE gold standard combination. CJC provides the GHRH signal; Ipamorelin provides the GHRP signal. Together = larger GH pulse than either alone, mimicking natural pulsatility.
- + Sleep optimization: GH released primarily during deep sleep. Taking before bed maximizes this.
- + Fasting: Low insulin state enhances GH response to CJC-1295
- + AOD-9604: CJC handles GH optimization and anti-aging; AOD handles targeted fat burning

**Cautions:**
- With DAC: Very long half-life means any side effects persist longer
- May cause water retention initially (normalize within 1–2 weeks)
- May cause transient fatigue or mild headache initially
- Do not use in active cancer, acromegaly, or uncontrolled diabetes
- Avoid if IGF-1 is already elevated

## Dihexa
**Class:** Angiotensin IV analog  
**Type:** Nootropic peptide

**Mechanism:**
Dihexa binds to HGF (hepatocyte growth factor) receptor c-Met and dramatically increases synaptic connectivity in the brain. It promotes the formation of new dendritic spines (the connection points between neurons). Animal studies show it is 10 million times more potent than BDNF for synaptic connection formation.

**Primary uses:**
- Cognitive enhancement
- Alzheimer's prevention/treatment (early research)
- Post-traumatic brain injury recovery
- Age-related cognitive decline

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 10–30 mg (note: milligrams, not micrograms — unusual for peptides)
Route: Oral or transdermal patch
Frequency: 2–3x per week (accumulates — cycling necessary)
Cycle: Use sparingly; 4–6 weeks max then extended break
```

**Cautions:**
- Very potent — start low
- Limited human data; most evidence from animal models
- Transdermal patch reduces risk of systemic overload
- Not for those with cancer history (HGF/c-Met pathway involved in cancer)

## Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide (DSIP)
**Class:** Neuropeptide  
**Type:** Sleep peptide

**Mechanism:**
DSIP is a naturally occurring neuropeptide that modulates sleep architecture. It decreases cortisol, reduces stress-induced locomotor activity, and promotes delta (deep) sleep specifically. Normalizes sleep patterns rather than simply sedating.

**Primary uses:**
- Chronic insomnia
- Stress-related sleep disruption
- Cortisol normalization
- Withdrawal support (opioid, alcohol)

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 100–300 mcg
Route: Subcutaneous or intranasal
Timing: 30–60 minutes before sleep
Frequency: As needed or 5 days on, 2 off
```

**Synergies:**
- + Magnesium citrate (MagicMag®): Magnesium supports ATP production during sleep; DSIP supports sleep architecture. Together address most sleep issues from two angles.
- + Tart cherry: Natural melatonin + DSIP = comprehensive sleep support

## Epithalon (Epitalon)
**Full name:** Epithalamin  
**Class:** Tetrapeptide (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly)  
**Type:** Longevity / telomere peptide

**Mechanism:**
Epithalon is derived from the epithalamus (pineal gland region). It activates telomerase — the enzyme responsible for maintaining and lengthening telomeres (the protective caps on chromosomes that shorten with each cell division). Longer telomeres = more cell divisions = slower biological aging.

Additional mechanisms:
- Regulates melatonin production (restores pineal function)
- Antioxidant via peroxidase activity normalization
- Modulates neuroendocrine function
- Anti-tumor properties in some models (unique for a telomerase activator)

**Primary uses:**
- Anti-aging / longevity protocols
- Sleep normalization (restores pineal melatonin rhythm)
- Immune function enhancement
- Reducing age-related hormonal decline
- Telomere support

**Protocol:**
```
Cycle 1 (Annual):
Dose: 5–10 mg/day
Duration: 10–20 consecutive days
Route: Subcutaneous injection or intranasal
Frequency: 2x/year (e.g., spring and fall)

Alternative (lower maintenance):
Dose: 2 mg every 3 days
Duration: Ongoing low-dose maintenance
```

**Synergies:**
- + NMN/NAD+: Epithalon extends telomeres; NAD+ repairs DNA and activates sirtuins. Together: most comprehensive anti-aging peptide stack.
- + GH peptides (CJC/Ipamorelin): GH declines with aging; restoring it alongside telomere support = multi-angle anti-aging
- + Resveratrol: Sirtuin activation synergizes with telomere protection

**Cautions:**
- Generally very well tolerated
- Limited long-term human safety data
- Given telomerase activation: theoretical concern in cancer (discuss with oncologist)
- Telomerase activators are a double-edged sword — context and health status matters

## Follistatin 344 / Follistatin 315
**Class:** Activin-binding glycoprotein  
**Type:** Muscle building / anti-myostatin peptide

**Mechanism:**
Follistatin binds and neutralizes myostatin (GDF-8) — the body's primary muscle growth inhibitor. By blocking myostatin, follistatin removes the brake on muscle development. Also binds activin A (suppresses FSH, affects fertility).

**Primary uses:**
- Maximum muscle hypertrophy
- Muscle wasting conditions (sarcopenia, post-surgery)
- Athletic performance

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 100 mcg every other day
Route: Intramuscular (injected into target muscle group)
Cycle: 10–30 days maximum, extended break required
Site-specific: Can be injected into lagging muscle groups for localized effect
```

**Cautions:**
- Short cycles only — longer use causes receptor desensitization and potential adverse hormonal effects
- May affect fertility (activin inhibition affects FSH)
- Limited human safety data
- High potential for misuse — not for casual use

**Metabolic connection:**

**Primary uses:**
- GH optimization
- Fat loss
- Muscle building
- Recovery

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 100–300 mcg
Frequency: 2–3x daily (before sleep mandatory)
Route: Subcutaneous injection
Combine with: CJC-1295 (without DAC) for synergy
Cycle: 3 months on, 1 month off
```

**Synergies:**
- Best combined with a GHRH analog (CJC-1295 without DAC or Sermorelin)
- + Ipamorelin: Less preferred (both are GHRPs — use GHRH + GHRP combination instead)

**Cautions:**
- May increase cortisol and prolactin (less than GHRP-6)
- Water retention possible initially
- Do not use with active cancer

**Primary uses:**
- Maximum GH stimulation (short-term use)
- Cardiac protection and recovery

**Protocol:**

- Receptor desensitization is faster than other GHRPs — must cycle
- Strongest GH pulse = most water retention risk
- More cortisol/prolactin elevation than Ipamorelin
- Cardioprotective effects make it interesting for cardiac patients — require medical supervision

## IGF-1 LR3 (Insulin-like Growth Factor 1 Long-Arg3)
**Class:** Modified insulin-like growth factor  
**Type:** Anabolic peptide

**Mechanism:**
IGF-1 LR3 is a modified version of natural IGF-1 with an extended half-life (~20–30 hours vs. 15 minutes for natural IGF-1). IGF-1 mediates many of GH's anabolic effects — it binds to IGF-1 receptors on muscle cells causing hyperplasia (new muscle cell creation) and hypertrophy (existing cell growth). Unlike insulin, it uses fat preferentially as fuel while building muscle.

**Primary uses:**
- Maximum muscle building
- Muscle hyperplasia (new fiber creation — unusual — most methods only cause hypertrophy)
- Recovery from injury with muscle loss
- Body composition optimization

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 20–60 mcg/day
Route: Intramuscular (into trained muscle for local effect) or subcutaneous
Timing: Immediately post-workout
Cycle: 4–6 weeks, then extended break (8+ weeks minimum)
Note: Do NOT use daily year-round — receptor desensitization and safety concerns
```

**Cautions:**
- One of the more powerful anabolic peptides — requires care
- Risk of hypoglycemia (similar to insulin) — have fast carbohydrates nearby
- Do not use if IGF-1 is already elevated
- Absolutely contraindicated with cancer — IGF-1 is a growth factor for cancer cells
- Rare: numbness/tingling (carpal tunnel-like) from fluid shifts

## Ipamorelin
**Class:** Synthetic pentapeptide  
**Type:** Selective GH secretagogue (GHRP)

**Mechanism:**
Ipamorelin is the most selective GHRP available. It stimulates GH release specifically from the pituitary via ghrelin receptor activation WITHOUT significantly raising cortisol, prolactin, ACTH, or aldosterone (the unwanted effects of older GHRPs). The selectivity makes it the cleanest, most favorable GHRP for most applications.

**Why Ipamorelin is preferred over other GHRPs:**
- GHRP-6: Causes hunger, cortisol increase, prolactin increase
- GHRP-2: Causes cortisol and prolactin increase
- Hexarelin: Fastest desensitization, more side effects
- Ipamorelin: Clean GH release only — the gold standard GHRP

**Primary uses:**
- GH optimization (anti-aging)
- Fat loss
- Muscle building and recovery
- Sleep quality improvement
- Injury recovery
- Most popular GHRP for general health and longevity

**Protocol:**
```
Standard protocol:
Dose: 100–300 mcg
Route: Subcutaneous injection
Timing: 30 minutes before sleep (most important dose)
Optional 2nd dose: Before workout or upon waking
Combine with: CJC-1295 without DAC (Mod GRF 1-29) — the gold standard combination
Cycle: 3 months on, 4 weeks off

Minimalist protocol:
Dose: 100 mcg before sleep only
Cycle: 6 months on, 6 weeks off
```

**Expected timeline:**
- Week 1–2: Better sleep, vivid dreams, slight water retention
- Week 3–4: More energy, faster recovery from workouts
- Week 6–8: Visible body composition improvements
- Month 3: Significant improvements in muscle:fat ratio, skin quality

**Synergies:**
- + CJC-1295 without DAC: THE combination. Use simultaneously.
- + AOD-9604: Complete GH optimization — Ipamorelin/CJC handles GH pulse and recovery; AOD handles targeted fat burning
- + Sleep optimization (magnesium + tart cherry): Deeper sleep = more GH released per pulse
- + Fasting: Low insulin maximizes GH response

**Cautions:**
- One of the safest GHRPs due to selectivity
- May cause mild water retention in first 1–2 weeks (resolves)
- Slight fatigue and headache possible in first week
- Do not use with active cancer or uncontrolled diabetes
- Not for pregnant or breastfeeding women

- Chronic infection (bacterial, fungal)


**Cautions:**

- Primarily used topically or at low injectable doses

## MOTS-c
**Full name:** Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the 12S rRNA-c  
**Class:** Mitochondrial-derived peptide  
**Type:** Metabolic regulator

**Mechanism:**
MOTS-c is encoded in mitochondrial DNA — one of the first mitochondrial-derived peptides discovered. It functions as a metabolic hormone: activating AMPK (the master metabolic switch), regulating glucose metabolism in muscle, and reducing insulin resistance. MOTS-c levels decline with age, obesity, and insulin resistance.

Key effects at molecular level:
- AMPK activation: mimics exercise and fasting at the cellular level
- AICAR production: activates the same pathway as metformin and berberine
- Folate pathway modulation: regulates one-carbon metabolism (critical for DNA synthesis and repair)
- Anti-inflammatory via NF-κB inhibition

**Primary uses:**
- Insulin resistance reversal
- Type 2 diabetes support
- Fat loss (especially in obesity)
- Exercise performance and endurance
- Healthy aging and longevity
- Metabolic disease treatment

**Protocol:**
```
Dose: 5–15 mg
Route: Subcutaneous injection
Frequency: 3–5x per week (or daily in metabolic disease)
Timing: Morning (aligns with natural circadian variation)
Cycle: Continuous use appears safe; 3-month cycles commonly used
```

**Synergies:**
- + Intermittent fasting: Both activate AMPK — synergistic metabolic activation
- + 3×1 diet: Low insulin from diet allows MOTS-c's insulin-sensitizing effects to compound
- + Berberine: Both activate AMPK through different mechanisms — potentially additive
- + Exercise (Zone 2 cardio): Exercise activates AMPK; MOTS-c sustains and amplifies the signal
- + NMN/NAD+: MOTS-c regulates mitochondrial function; NAD+ is mitochondrial fuel. Together: comprehensive mitochondrial health.

**Cautions:**
- Very well tolerated in available research
- Emerging peptide — limited long-term human data
- Monitor blood glucose if diabetic (significant glucose-lowering effect)
- May interact with diabetes medications

**Metabolic connection:**
MOTS-c is the most metabolically relevant peptide in this glossary. It directly addresses insulin resistance, fat burning, and mitochondrial function — the three root causes of metabolic disease. It is essentially an exercise and fasting mimetic at the cellular level.

## PT-141 (Bremelanotide)
**Class:** Melanocortin agonist  
**Type:** Sexual function peptide

- Hypoactive sexual desire disorder (low libido) in men and women

- Blood pressure: Causes transient blood pressure increase (typically +6 mmHg systolic for 12 hours) — caution with hypertension
- Nausea: Common especially at higher doses
- Flushing
- FDA-approved version (Vyleesi) is injected into abdomen or thigh

## Sermorelin
**Class:** GH-releasing hormone analog (first 29 amino acids of natural GHRH)  
**Type:** GH secretagogue

**Mechanism:**
Sermorelin is a truncated version of natural GHRH (44 amino acids → 29 amino acids). It stimulates the pituitary gland to produce and release GH in a pulsatile, physiological manner — restoring the natural GH decline of aging without introducing exogenous GH. Unlike direct GH injection, Sermorelin doesn't suppress the body's own GH production and is self-limiting (won't raise GH above physiological ranges).

**Primary uses:**

# APPENDIX: Peptide Administration Guide

## Reconstitution (Lyophilized Powder → Injectable)

```
Supplies needed:
- Bacteriostatic water (NOT sterile water — bacteriostatic contains benzyl alcohol preservative)
- Insulin syringe (29-31 gauge, 0.5 mL or 1 mL)
- Alcohol swabs
- The peptide vial

Procedure:
1. Wipe rubber stopper of both vials with alcohol swab
2. Draw the specified amount of bacteriostatic water into syringe
   (e.g., for 5 mg vial: add 2 mL bac water → 1 mL = 2.5 mg = 2,500 mcg)
3. Inject water SLOWLY down the SIDE of the peptide vial (not directly onto powder)
4. DO NOT shake — gently swirl until dissolved
5. The solution should be clear and colorless
6. Store reconstituted vial in refrigerator (2–8°C)
7. Use within 30 days
```

## Calculating Doses

```
Example: 5 mg vial + 2 mL bacteriostatic water
= 5,000 mcg in 2 mL
= 2,500 mcg per mL
= 250 mcg per 0.1 mL

To inject 250 mcg: Draw to the 10-unit mark on a 100-unit (1 mL) insulin syringe
To inject 500 mcg: Draw to the 20-unit mark
```

## Injection Technique (Subcutaneous)

```
Sites: Abdomen (2 inches from navel), outer thigh, upper arm
Technique:
1. Pinch skin between thumb and forefinger
2. Insert needle at 45-90 degree angle
3. Inject slowly (over 5-10 seconds)
4. Remove needle, apply light pressure (don't rub)
5. Rotate sites — never inject same spot consecutively
```

## Storage Guide

| State | Temperature | Duration |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Lyophilized (dry) powder | Room temperature | 6–12 months |
| Lyophilized in refrigerator | 4°C | 2+ years |
| Reconstituted (liquid) | 4°C | 20–30 days |
| Reconstituted, frozen | -20°C | Several months (avoid repeated freeze-thaw) |
