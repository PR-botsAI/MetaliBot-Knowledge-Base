# 🥥 Coconut Oil — MCT Science

## What Makes Coconut Oil Different

Most dietary fats are **long-chain triglycerides (LCTs)** — they require bile salts for digestion, enter the lymphatic system, and can be stored as body fat.

Coconut oil is rich in **medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs)** — a fundamentally different fat that behaves more like a carbohydrate in terms of energy use:

| Property | Long-Chain Fats | MCTs (Coconut Oil) |
|----------|----------------|--------------------|
| Digestion | Requires bile salts | Bypasses normal fat digestion |
| Absorption route | Lymphatic system | Directly to portal vein → liver |
| Energy availability | Slow | Immediate |
| Storage tendency | High | Very low |
| Brain fuel | Limited | Efficient (ketones) |

## The Antifungal Property

Coconut oil contains **lauric acid** (and to lesser extents caprylic and capric acid), which have **well-documented antifungal properties** with specific activity against Candida albicans.

Mechanism: Lauric acid disrupts the lipid membrane of Candida cells, killing them without harming human cells.

This makes coconut oil a **dual-action ally** in metabolic health:
1. ✅ Provides instant, clean energy
2. ✅ Actively fights Candida overgrowth

## Practical Uses

**For cooking:**
- Excellent high-heat cooking oil (stable at high temperatures — doesn't oxidize)
- Replaces vegetable oils (soybean, canola, sunflower — all problematic)
- Can replace butter in most applications

**For Candida protocol:**
- 1-2 tablespoons per day, incorporated into diet
- Can be added to coffee, smoothies, or taken directly
- Morning use recommended for energy

**For athletes/active people:**
- Pre-workout: provides immediate fuel without glucose spike
- Supports ketone production for sustained energy

✅ **Use:** Virgin or extra virgin coconut oil, cold-pressed

❌ **Avoid:** Refined, deodorized, or hydrogenated coconut oil
