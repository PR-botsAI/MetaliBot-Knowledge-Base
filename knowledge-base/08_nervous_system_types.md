# 🧠 Nervous System Types — Metabolic Personalization

## The Core Insight

One diet does NOT fit all bodies.

The autonomic nervous system — which regulates metabolism, digestion, heart rate, and hormones — has two primary modes. Your dominant mode determines which foods are YOUR right fuel.

**The engine analogy:**
> If you put diesel fuel in a gasoline engine, it won't run properly — not because something is wrong with the engine or the fuel. It's the wrong fuel for that engine.

Eating the wrong foods for your nervous system type produces the same result: no energy, regardless of how "healthy" the food is.

## The Two Types

### EXCITADO TYPE (Sympathetic Dominant)

**Profile:**
- High-energy, accelerated, reactive
- Emotionally sensitive
- Easily overstimulated

**Digestion:** SENSITIVE — cannot tolerate heavy fats or large protein loads easily

**RIGHT fuel:**
- More vegetables and salads
- Lighter meals overall
- Calming foods
- Moderate carbohydrates (they have a calming effect for this type)

**WRONG fuel:**
- Heavy, fatty meals
- Protein overload
- Stimulating foods

**Warning signs when eating wrong:**
- Anxiety increases after eating
- Fast or irregular heart rate
- Insomnia worsens
- Nausea or discomfort after heavy meals
- Digestive issues (reflux, bloating)

**Important note:** Some carbohydrates are genuinely calming for excitado types. Don't completely eliminate them — they serve a nervous system regulation function for this body type.

---

### PASIVO TYPE (Parasympathetic Dominant)

**Profile:**
- Calmer, slower, lower baseline energy
- Even-keeled emotionally
- Can seem sluggish or unmotivated

**Digestion:** STRONG — handles more variety without difficulty

**RIGHT fuel:**
- Higher protein
- More healthy fats
- Stimulating, activating foods

**WRONG fuel:**
- Excess refined carbohydrates (MORE damaging for this type than any other)
- Heavy starchy meals that induce further sluggishness

**Warning signs when eating wrong:**
- Low energy throughout the day
- Sluggishness after meals
- Weight gain even when "eating healthy"
- Can fall asleep almost anywhere
- Low motivation or drive

---

## The Food-Emotion-Behavior Connection

Food **directly** affects mood and behavior through:
1. Nervous system stimulation or sedation
2. Hormone release (cortisol, serotonin, dopamine)
3. Blood sugar fluctuations

When a person eats against their nervous system type:
- Irritability without apparent reason
- Anxiety after meals
- Inability to focus
- Energy crashes
- Mood swings

**Especially in children:** Behavioral issues (hyperactivity, ADHD-like symptoms, emotional outbursts) often trace directly to foods mismatched with the child's nervous system type — not to behavioral disorders.

## How to Identify Your Type

**Excitado indicators:**
- Tend toward anxiety or worry
- Fast talker, fast thinker
- Light sleeper
- Feel worse after heavy, fatty meals
- Sensitive to stress
- Heart rate tends to be faster

**Pasivo indicators:**
- Tend toward low energy or sluggishness
- Calmer, more deliberate pace
- Can sleep easily and anywhere
- Feel heavy or more tired after large meals
- Weight comes on easily
- Heart rate tends to be slower

**Most people are a mix** with one dominant type. The dietary adjustments don't need to be extreme — moderate shifts toward the right fuel make a significant difference.

## Application in Practice

| Situation | Excitado Approach | Pasivo Approach |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Breakfast | Lighter — eggs + vegetables | Protein-heavy — eggs + meat |
| Lunch | Salad-focused with moderate protein | Meat-focused with vegetables |
| Dinner | Calming — vegetables + moderate protein | Satisfying — protein + fat + vegetables |
| Snacks | Nuts, light protein | Cheese, meat, eggs |
| Carbs | Small amounts help calm the system | Minimize — they worsen sluggishness |
