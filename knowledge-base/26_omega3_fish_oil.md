# 🐟 Omega-3 Fatty Acids & Fish Oil — Complete Science Guide

> Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats the body cannot produce — they must come from diet. They are among the most extensively studied nutrients in medicine, with documented effects on inflammation, cardiovascular health, brain function, insulin resistance, and metabolic health.

---

## The Omega-3 vs. Omega-6 Balance — Why It Matters

**The ratio problem in modern diet:**
- Ancestral human diet: Omega-6 to Omega-3 ratio ≈ 1:1 to 4:1
- Modern Western diet: ratio ≈ 15:1 to 25:1
- This massive imbalance is a primary driver of chronic inflammation

**Why this ratio matters:**
- Omega-6 (linoleic acid, arachidonic acid): Pro-inflammatory when in excess
- Omega-3 (EPA, DHA, ALA): Anti-inflammatory, compete for the same enzymes
- When omega-6 dominates: inflammatory cascade is amplified; omega-3 cannot compete
- Restoring ratio toward 4:1 or better dramatically reduces systemic inflammation

**Major omega-6 sources to REDUCE:**
- Soybean oil (most common oil in processed food)
- Corn oil
- Canola oil
- Sunflower/safflower oil
- Cottonseed oil
- Any "vegetable oil"

## The Three Main Omega-3s

### ALA (Alpha-Linolenic Acid)
- **Source:** Plant foods — flaxseed, chia seeds, walnuts, hemp seeds
- **Limitation:** Must be converted to EPA and DHA to be fully useful
- **Conversion efficiency:** Only 5–10% converts to EPA, less than 1% to DHA
- **Conclusion:** ALA from plants is NOT an adequate substitute for direct EPA/DHA from marine sources for most people

### EPA (Eicosapentaenoic Acid)
- **Source:** Fatty fish, fish oil, krill oil, algae oil
- **Primary role:** Anti-inflammatory signaling. EPA is the dominant anti-inflammatory omega-3.
- **Key actions:**
  - Reduces triglycerides (FDA-approved at 4g/day for hypertriglyceridemia)
  - Competes with arachidonic acid to reduce inflammatory prostaglandins
  - Reduces platelet aggregation (mild blood thinning)
  - Supports mood and reduces depression
  - Reduces inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6)

### DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid)
- **Source:** Fatty fish, fish oil, krill oil, algae oil (most direct plant-based source)
- **Primary role:** Structural component of brain and retina
- **Key actions:**
  - 30-40% of brain gray matter fatty acid composition is DHA
  - Required for fetal brain development (critical during pregnancy)
  - Supports neuronal membrane fluidity and signal transmission
  - Protects against Alzheimer's and cognitive decline
  - Reduces triglycerides
  - Anti-inflammatory

## Clinical Evidence — What Omega-3s Actually Do

### Cardiovascular Health
- **Triglycerides:** Reduces by 15–30% at 2–4g/day (dose-dependent)
- **Blood pressure:** Reduces systolic by ~4 mmHg and diastolic by ~3 mmHg
- **Plaque stability:** Makes atherosclerotic plaques more stable (less likely to rupture)
- **Arrhythmia:** Reduces risk of fatal ventricular arrhythmia
- **Heart failure:** Reduces cardiovascular mortality
- **ASCVD prevention:** High-dose EPA (Vascepa — 4g/day) reduced major cardiovascular events by 25% in the REDUCE-IT trial

### Metabolic Health & Insulin Resistance
- **Triglycerides:** Directly relevant — high triglycerides are a marker of insulin resistance and refined carb excess
- **Fatty liver:** Reduces liver fat accumulation and inflammation (NAFLD/NASH)
- **Insulin sensitivity:** Improves insulin sensitivity in insulin-resistant individuals
- **Adiponectin:** Increases adiponectin (anti-inflammatory fat-burning hormone)
- **Inflammation:** Reduces CRP, TNF-α, IL-6 — all elevated in insulin resistance

### Brain & Mental Health
- **Depression:** Multiple meta-analyses show EPA-dominant formulas reduce depression. 1–2g EPA/day comparable to antidepressants in mild-moderate depression.
- **Anxiety:** Reduces anxiety symptoms. A 2018 meta-analysis found 14% reduction in anxiety.
- **ADHD:** Reduces ADHD symptoms in children — some research suggests omega-3 deficiency is a contributing factor
- **Alzheimer's prevention:** DHA supplementation associated with slower cognitive decline
- **Postpartum depression:** DHA critical during pregnancy and breastfeeding for both mother and infant brain health

### Anti-Inflammatory Effects
- Reduces CRP (C-reactive protein) — marker of systemic inflammation
- Reduces IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β — major pro-inflammatory cytokines
- Resolves inflammation via specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs: resolvins, protectins)
- This "resolution" effect is as important as anti-inflammatory — omega-3s help TURN OFF inflammation, not just suppress it

### Joint Health
- Reduces rheumatoid arthritis symptoms — reduces morning stiffness, tender joints
- 3g/day fish oil comparable to anti-inflammatory medication in some RA studies
- Slows cartilage degradation

## Best Sources of Omega-3s

### Food Sources (by EPA+DHA content per 3.5 oz)

| Fish | EPA+DHA (g) | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Mackerel | 5.1g | Highest. Small fish = less mercury. |
| Salmon (wild) | 2.2–2.5g | Wild > farmed for omega-3 ratio |
| Sardines | 2.2g | Small fish, low mercury, very high omega-3 |
| Herring | 2.0g | Excellent, underused |
| Anchovies | 1.5g | Used in Caesar dressing — very good source |
| Trout | 1.0g | Good freshwater option |
| Tuna (bluefin) | 1.6g | Mercury concern — limit frequency |
| Tuna (canned, light) | 0.3g | Low mercury but low omega-3 |
| Shrimp | 0.3g | Common aggressor — check personal tolerance |

**Rule:** Smaller fish = more omega-3, less mercury. Larger predatory fish (tuna, swordfish, shark) = less omega-3, more mercury.

### Supplement Sources

**Fish Oil:**
- Most studied and used supplement form
- Triglyceride form (labeled TG or rTG) absorbs 50% better than ethyl ester (EE) form
- Look for: "re-esterified triglyceride" or just "triglyceride form"
- Key: total EPA+DHA per capsule (not just "fish oil" content)

**Krill Oil:**
- EPA/DHA bound to phospholipids (better brain uptake, more bioavailable)
- Contains astaxanthin (powerful antioxidant that protects the omega-3s from oxidation)
- Lower dose needed due to better absorption (500mg krill ≈ 1500mg fish oil)
- More expensive

**Algae Oil:**
- Direct vegan source of DHA (and some EPA)
- Fish get their omega-3s from algae — algae oil cuts out the middleman
- Best choice for: vegans/vegetarians, those avoiding fish
- Tends to be lower in EPA

## Dosing Protocols

### General Health & Prevention
- 1–2g EPA+DHA/day
- Food-based if possible (2–3 servings fatty fish/week)

### Inflammation, Joint Health, Metabolic Support
- 2–4g EPA+DHA/day
- EPA-dominant formula for inflammation
- Take with meals (fat-soluble — absorbs better with food)

### High Triglycerides (clinical range >500 mg/dL)
- 4g EPA+DHA/day (prescription dose)
- Reduces triglycerides 20–50%
- Consult physician — FDA-approved prescription omega-3 available (Vascepa, Lovaza)

### Depression/Mental Health
- 1–2g EPA/day specifically (DHA less important for mood)
- EPA-dominant formula (>60% EPA)

### Pregnancy
- 200–300mg DHA minimum (most OBs now recommend this)
- Supports fetal brain and eye development
- Reduces risk of preterm birth

### Athletes/Recovery
- 3–4g EPA+DHA/day
- Reduces DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness)
- Improves muscle protein synthesis response to resistance training

## Omega-3 + Metabolic Health Protocol Synergy

**How omega-3s amplify the metabolic protocol:**

1. **Triglycerides:** High triglycerides are a direct sign of insulin resistance and refined carb excess. 3×1 diet reduces them; omega-3 directly reduces them further — double action.
2. **Fatty liver:** Both the 3×1 diet and omega-3s independently reduce liver fat — synergistic.
3. **Inflammation:** Candida toxins + insulin resistance = chronic inflammation. Omega-3s reduce the same cytokines Candida elevates.
4. **Insulin sensitivity:** Both omega-3s and magnesium improve insulin sensitivity — different mechanisms, additive effect.
5. **Blood pressure:** Omega-3s reduce BP; potassium and magnesium reduce BP — three independent mechanisms working together.

## Quality, Freshness & Oxidation

**The biggest omega-3 problem: rancidity.**

Omega-3s are highly polyunsaturated — they oxidize easily. Oxidized fish oil is WORSE than no fish oil (produces harmful lipid peroxides).

**How to ensure quality:**
- Open a capsule and smell/taste — should taste slightly fishy but NOT rancid/paint-like
- Look for third-party testing (IFOS certification, ConsumerLab)
- Store in refrigerator once opened
- Smaller bottles (fresher) vs. large economy sizes
- Molecularly distilled (removes mercury, PCBs, dioxins)
- Look for antioxidants added: astaxanthin, Vitamin E (tocopherols)

**Reputable brands:** Nordic Naturals, Carlson, Thorne, Life Extension, Vital Choice

## Important Interactions & Cautions

- **Blood thinners (warfarin/Coumadin):** Omega-3s have mild antiplatelet effects — inform physician if on anticoagulants. Generally safe at ≤3g/day but requires monitoring.
- **Diabetes medications:** May enhance glucose-lowering effects — monitor blood glucose
- **Blood pressure medications:** Additive BP-lowering — may require dose adjustment
- **Surgery:** Some physicians recommend stopping 1 week before surgery due to antiplatelet effects
- **High doses (>4g/day):** Possible increase in LDL cholesterol (though this is debated and context-dependent)
