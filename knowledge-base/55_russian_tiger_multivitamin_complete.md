## Beta-Carotene — 25,000 IU (500% DV)

**What it is:** Pro-vitamin A — a plant-based carotenoid that the body converts to vitamin A as needed. Unlike preformed vitamin A, beta-carotene is self-limiting (excess is not converted, so toxicity risk is minimal).

**Mechanisms:**
- Converts to retinol (Vitamin A) via beta-carotene 15,15'-monooxygenase enzyme
- Antioxidant: Quenches singlet oxygen and peroxyl radicals — particularly protective in lipid environments (cell membranes, LDL particles)
- Immune support: Enhances natural killer cell activity and lymphocyte proliferation
- Skin protection: Concentrates in skin, providing photoprotection against UV damage
- Eye health: Precursor to rhodopsin (visual pigment in rods — night vision)

**Clinical applications:** Immune function, skin health, eye protection, antioxidant support.

**Note:** Heavy smokers should NOT take high-dose beta-carotene supplements (ATBC and CARET trials showed increased lung cancer risk in smokers at supplemental doses).

**Caution:** At 15,000 IU preformed vitamin A, this is a high dose. Chronic intake >10,000 IU/day of preformed vitamin A can cause toxicity (headache, liver damage, bone loss). ## Vitamin B12 — 250mcg (4,167% DV)

**What it is:** Cobalamin — the only vitamin containing a metal ion (cobalt). Cannot be made by plants or animals — produced exclusively by bacteria.

- Cyanocobalamin: Cheapest, requires conversion, less bioavailable for some

- Hydroxocobalamin: Injectable form, longest-lasting

## Vitamin B-17 (Amygdalin/Laetrile) — Listed on Front Label

**What it is:** Amygdalin — a cyanogenic glucoside found in apple seeds, apricot kernels, bitter almonds, and many other seeds. Note: "Vitamin B17" is NOT a recognized vitamin — this designation was created by alternative health advocates.

**Claimed mechanisms:**
- When metabolized: releases hydrogen cyanide (HCN), benzaldehyde, and glucuronic acid
- Theory: Cancer cells contain more beta-glucosidase enzyme which releases the HCN, selectively killing cancer cells. Normal cells have more rhodanese enzyme which neutralizes HCN.
- Apricot kernel extract is the primary source

**Scientific status:** The FDA banned laetrile for cancer treatment in the 1970s. Multiple clinical trials showed it was ineffective for cancer treatment. Significant cyanide toxicity cases documented, including deaths from high-dose amygdalin.

**At trace amounts in a multivitamin:** The quantity is likely negligible and the cyanide concern is minimal at the doses present in a multi.

**Note:** The inclusion of "Vitamin B-17" is largely a marketing distinction for the alternative health community, and the dose in this formula is not pharmacologically significant for cancer treatment (nor is it effective for that purpose).
