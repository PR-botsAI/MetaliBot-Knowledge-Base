## What Is Type 2 Diabetes — The Real Explanation

Diabetes is NOT primarily a sugar problem. It is an **insulin signaling failure** caused by years of metabolic abuse.

The progression:
```
Years of excess refined carbohydrates
  → Chronically high insulin
  → Cells destroy their own insulin receptors (downregulation)
  → Insulin can no longer deliver glucose into cells
  → Glucose piles up in the bloodstream
  = TYPE 2 DIABETES
```

The diabetic has TOO MUCH glucose AND TOO MUCH insulin in the blood simultaneously — but the insulin doesn't work because the cells stopped listening.

## The Obesity-Diabetes Connection

**Clinical fact:** More than **85% of Type 2 diabetics are overweight.**

This is not a coincidence — obesity and diabetes share the same root cause:
- Excess refined carbohydrates → excess insulin → insulin resistance → obesity AND diabetes
- Both conditions create and worsen each other in a loop
- **Control obesity → control diabetes.** This is the metabolic approach.

## The 3×1 Diet for Diabetes Control

The plate proportion system directly addresses both conditions simultaneously:

**Type S Foods (Supporting for diabetes, Slimming):**
All meats, fish, seafood, eggs, cheese, avocado, olive oil, coconut oil, butter, nuts, all vegetables that grow above ground.

**Type F Foods (Foes for diabetes, Fattening — limit to 25% of plate):**
Bread, pasta, rice, tortillas, potato, sweet potato, yuca, plantain, cereals, sugar, honey, sweets, chocolate, milk, fruit juices, sodas.

**Why this works for diabetics:**
Reducing Type F foods → less glucose entering bloodstream → pancreas produces less insulin → cells regain insulin sensitivity over time → blood glucose normalizes.

Many diabetics using this approach have reduced or eliminated insulin injections and oral medications — always under medical supervision.

## Blood Glucose Targets

| Status | Fasting Glucose | After Meals |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| Non-diabetic normal | 70–99 mg/dL | Under 140 mg/dL |
| Prediabetes | 100–125 mg/dL | — |
| Diabetic | 130+ mg/dL tends to persist | 180+ mg/dL |
| Optimal diabetic target | 70–130 mg/dL always | Stay under 140 |

**The A1c test:** Measures average glucose control over 3 months. Goal: 6.0 or less. Should be done every 3–6 months.

## The Glucometer: Your Most Important Tool

A glucometer measures blood glucose instantly from a fingertip drop of blood.

**How to use it for food testing (the 12-point rule):**
1. Measure glucose BEFORE eating
2. Eat only the food you want to test
3. Measure again exactly 1 hour later
4. **Rise of more than 12 points = that food is an aggressor for YOUR body**

This gives you objective, personalized data about which foods spike YOUR glucose — not a generic list.

**When to measure:**
- Upon waking (fasting glucose)
- 1–2 hours after meals (postprandial glucose)
- Before and after exercise
- When feeling symptoms (dizziness, fatigue, confusion)

## Candida and Diabetes — The Hidden Connection

This connection is almost never discussed in conventional diabetes care:

- The vast majority of diabetics carry a **severe Candida yeast overgrowth**
- Candida feeds directly on glucose and refined carbohydrates
- Candida toxins create systemic inflammation that **worsens insulin resistance**
- This creates a loop: high glucose feeds Candida → Candida worsens insulin resistance → insulin resistance raises glucose → more Candida

**The metabolic approach to diabetes MUST include Candida control.** Without it, blood sugar control is much harder to achieve.

## Magnesium and Diabetes

Over 20 clinical studies demonstrate that magnesium deficiency makes it impossible to control diabetes:

- The pancreatic beta cells that produce insulin **cannot function** without magnesium
- Magnesium deficiency causes beta cells to lose ability to produce functional insulin
- Insulin receptors are structurally built from magnesium — deficiency = broken receptors
- Supplementing with magnesium citrate directly supports insulin function and glucose control

**This is why the 3×1 diet + magnesium + Candida cleanse is such a powerful combination for diabetics.**

## Fatty Liver and Diabetes

Almost all Type 2 diabetics have some degree of fatty liver:

- Excess glucose/fructose → liver converts it to fat → fatty liver
- Fatty liver → liver cannot process glucose efficiently → hyperglycemia worsens
- Fatty liver → liver cannot convert T4→T3 → hypothyroid symptoms + slow metabolism
- Fatty liver + insulin resistance form a **self-reinforcing loop**

**Clinical observation:** When patients clean their fatty liver, blood glucose improves dramatically — often allowing medication reduction.

## Complications of Uncontrolled Diabetes

High blood glucose (above 130 mg/dL consistently) causes **glycation** — glucose fuses to cell proteins and destroys them:

| Complication | What Happens |
|-------------|-------------|
| Diabetic neuropathy | Nerve damage — numbness in feet/legs, sexual dysfunction |
| Nephropathy | Kidney damage leading to dialysis |
| Retinopathy | Eye damage leading to blindness |
| Cardiovascular | Heart attack, stroke, atherosclerosis |
| Peripheral arterial | Poor circulation → wounds that don't heal → amputation |
| Alzheimer's | Sometimes called "brain diabetes" — significantly higher incidence |

**The good news:** These complications are largely preventable and in early stages reversible by controlling glucose through metabolic restoration.

## What Makes Glucose Control Harder

**Factors that raise glucose even without eating:**
- Stress (cortisol triggers liver to release stored glucose)
- Poor sleep
- Candida toxin load (creates insulin resistance)
- Magnesium deficiency (impairs insulin function)
- Dehydration (concentrates blood glucose)
- Hypothyroidism / fatty liver (slows all metabolic processes)
- Certain medications (steroids, some antidepressants)

**Factors that improve glucose control:**
- Reducing Type F foods (3×1 system)
- Adequate hydration
- Magnesium + potassium supplementation
- Candida cleanse
- Walking / physical activity
- Stress reduction
- Quality sleep

---

## Exercise and Diabetes

Physical activity — especially walking — directly lowers blood glucose by:
1. Muscles use glucose directly without requiring insulin during exercise
2. After exercise, muscles continue absorbing glucose for recovery
3. Regular exercise improves insulin sensitivity over time
4. Reduces cortisol (stress hormone that raises glucose)

**Recommendation:** 30+ minutes of walking daily. This is one of the most powerful natural glucose-lowering interventions.

## Prediabetes — The Warning Stage

Fasting glucose of 100–125 mg/dL = prediabetes.

Many people are prediabetic for **8–10 years before officially being diagnosed** as diabetic. The damage is happening during those years.

**This is the easiest time to reverse the condition** — before the cells have lost most of their receptors. Applying the 3×1 diet + magnesium + hydration + Candida control at this stage can completely prevent Type 2 diabetes from developing.
