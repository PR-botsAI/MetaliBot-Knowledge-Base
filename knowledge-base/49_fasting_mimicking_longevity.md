# ⏳ Fasting Mimicking Diet & Longevity Science

> The Fasting Mimicking Diet (FMD) was developed by Dr. Valter Longo at the USC Longevity Institute. It provides the health benefits of extended fasting while allowing food intake — making it more accessible and safer than complete fasting for most people.

## What Is the Fasting Mimicking Diet

**The FMD is a 5-day protocol:**

| Day | Calories | Macros |
|-----|---------|--------|
| Day 1 | 1,090 kcal | 10% protein, 56% fat, 34% carbs |
| Days 2–5 | 725 kcal each | 9% protein, 44% fat, 47% carbs |

**Key nutritional principles:**
- Very low protein (critical — protein activates mTOR and blunts fasting effects)
- Low but not zero carbohydrates
- Moderate healthy fat
- Plant-based composition (predominantly)
- Specific micronutrient supplementation included

**Why this works while eating:**
- Very low protein → minimal mTOR activation → autophagy proceeds
- Very low overall calories → glycogen depletion → metabolic switch
- The body cannot tell the difference (metabolically) between this protocol and a 5-day water fast in many key metrics

## What Happens During the FMD — By Day

**Day 1 – Transition:**
- Body begins depleting glycogen stores
- Insulin and IGF-1 drop significantly
- Mild hunger as body adjusts

**Days 2–3 – Fasting State:**
- Ketone production increases
- Autophagy significantly elevated
- Growth hormone rises (protective of muscle)
- Stem cell activity begins increasing
- Fat burning dominant

**Days 4–5 – Deep Fasting State:**
- Maximum autophagy activation
- Stem cell regeneration
- Immune cell rejuvenation
- Significant ketone levels
- Many people report mental clarity

**Days 6–7 (refeeding) – Regeneration:**
- Coming off the FMD, stem cells activated during the fast begin differentiating
- This is when regeneration actually happens — the fast creates the signal; refeeding provides the materials
- Careful refeeding is important: start light, build up over 1–2 days

## Clinical Evidence

### Human Trials (Longo et al., Science Translational Medicine, 2017)
**100 adults, 3 monthly FMD cycles:**
- Reduced body weight (2.6kg average)
- Reduced waist circumference
- Reduced blood pressure
- Reduced IGF-1 (longevity marker — lower IGF-1 = longer lifespan in most research)
- Reduced fasting glucose
- Reduced cholesterol
- Reduced triglycerides
- Reduced CRP (inflammation)
- **No negative effects on muscle mass** (unlike caloric restriction which does reduce muscle)

### Longevity Mechanisms
**From animal research:**
- FMD extended lifespan by 11% in middle-aged mice
- Reduced cancer incidence
- Reduced inflammatory disease
- Improved cognitive function in aged mice
- Reversed some age-related markers (treated aged mice looked metabolically younger)

### Type 2 Diabetes and Metabolic Disease
- Reduces fasting glucose significantly
- Improves insulin sensitivity
- Reduces A1c
- Weight loss concentrated in visceral (metabolically harmful) fat
- The 5-day protocol produces benefits equivalent to much longer lifestyle interventions in some metrics

### Autoimmune Conditions
- Studies in multiple sclerosis: reduced disease activity
- The immune regeneration mechanism (old immune cells destroyed via autophagy; new ones generated during refeeding) may explain this

## DIY FMD Protocol

*ProLon® (the commercial product) is expensive (~$200/cycle). A DIY version produces similar results:*

**Day 1 (1,100 calories):**
```
Breakfast: Vegetable soup with olive oil (300 cal)
Lunch: Large green salad, olive oil dressing, handful of nuts (300 cal)
Dinner: Vegetable soup + steamed vegetables + olive oil (300 cal)
Snacks: 5–10 olives + handful of macadamia nuts (200 cal)
Supplement: Multivitamin, fish oil, electrolytes
```

**Days 2–5 (725 calories each):**
```
Breakfast: Herbal tea + a small handful of nuts (100 cal)
Lunch: Vegetable soup (no potato, no beans) + olive oil (250 cal)
Dinner: Steamed vegetables + olive oil + small portion cooked quinoa (250 cal)
Snack: 5 olives or cucumber slices (50 cal)
Fluids: Herbal teas, water, electrolytes, black coffee/tea (optional)
Supplements: B vitamins, electrolytes throughout
```

**Refeeding (Days 6–7):**
```
Day 6: Light, mostly plant-based. Small portions. Avoid large protein or fat loads.
Day 7: Gradual return to normal. First protein meal.
No heavy meals or alcohol for at least 24 hours post-FMD.
```

**Frequency:**
- For metabolic disease treatment: monthly for 3–6 months
- For healthy aging: once every 3–6 months
- For weight loss: monthly until goal achieved, then quarterly maintenance

## FMD Cautions

**Contraindications (do not do without medical supervision):**
- Underweight (BMI <18.5)
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Type 1 diabetes
- Taking insulin or sulfonylurea medications (hypoglycemia risk)
- Active cancer treatment
- Eating disorders history
- Children or adolescents

**Requires medical supervision:**
- Type 2 diabetes on any medication
- Any heart condition
- Any advanced disease

## The Longevity Science Framework

**Dr. Longo's Longevity Diet (not just FMD — the everyday eating pattern):**
- Mostly plant-based but not necessarily vegan
- Fish (small, low-mercury) 2–3x/week
- Low to moderate protein: 0.31–0.36g per pound of body weight
- High complex carbohydrates (whole grains, legumes)
- High in healthy fats: olive oil, nuts, avocado
- Low in added sugar
- No red or processed meat
- Eat within 12-hour window (mild circadian restriction)
- 5-day FMD monthly or quarterly

**Note on protein:** Longo advocates lower protein than many current recommendations, especially for middle-aged adults (35–65). Research shows low protein extends lifespan in multiple organisms. HOWEVER: adults over 65 may need higher protein to maintain muscle mass. Context matters.
